# Zobo Jobs Recruitment Agency Marketing Video

## Style Block

Use the COLLINS identity from `DESIGN.md`: Canvas Parchment `#f8f8f7`, Absolute White `#ffffff`, Deep Onyx `#140700`, Pewter Accent `#cccccc`, Slate Echo `#5e5855`, and Shadowed Steel `#514c49`. Keep the palette achromatic and high contrast. Typography should reference Portrait Text / Times for display authority and Graphik / Arial / system sans for operational labels. The mood is architectural parchment, editorial restraint, and precise commercial proof.

## Rhythm Declaration

Pattern: `hook-build-PUNCH-breathe-proof-CTA`, 28 seconds total. The energy starts editorial and tense, builds through workflow motion, peaks on the economic argument, then resolves with evidence and a direct agency-facing call to action.

## Global Rules

Use a light parchment canvas with visible structure, not empty whitespace: oversized ghost typography, rules, ledger lines, panel borders, and masked product imagery. Treat transitions as page turns and focus shifts: blur crossfades, vertical pushes, and one decisive dark-band wipe into the CTA. Every scene needs multiple focal points, large readable type, and animated foreground accents.

## Scene Beats

### 1. Agencies Are Still Paying For First Rounds

Concept: Open on a recruitment agency pipeline that feels expensive before a single hire is made. The viewer should feel the drag of calendars, repeated screening calls, and client pressure.

Mood direction: Editorial audit report meets premium consultancy reel.

Depth layers: BG parchment, oversized ghost word `SCREENING`, ledger rules, corner registration marks. MG large serif headline and right-side cost/time ledger. FG thin divider, small `agency ops` label, moving scan line.

Animation choreography: Headline slides in with weight, ledger rows cascade, scan line draws, registration marks stamp into place.

Transition out: Blur crossfade with a vertical push, 0.45s, `power2.inOut`.

### 2. AI Interviews Every Candidate

Concept: The manual bottleneck turns into an always-on interview layer. Every candidate gets a consistent, structured first conversation without waiting for recruiter availability.

Mood direction: Calm operating system, precise and human-facing.

Depth layers: BG parchment with faint network lines, MG masked AI interviewer image and candidate cards, FG status chips and rule markers.

Animation choreography: Image reveals upward, candidate cards cascade, status chips fill, connecting lines draw.

Transition out: Editorial page slide, 0.4s, `power3.inOut`.

### 3. Save Time And Cost Before Shortlist

Concept: Make the commercial case concrete: fewer manual screens, lower coordination overhead, faster client-ready shortlist. The beat should feel like a cost model tightening.

Mood direction: Finance memo, boardroom proof, no hype.

Depth layers: BG ledger grid, MG three large stat blocks, FG progress bars, small formula text, moving tick marks.

Animation choreography: Numbers count up, progress bars fill, formula snaps into focus, ticks mark the savings.

Transition out: Dark onyx band wipe, 0.38s, `power4.inOut`.

### 4. Hire The Best Candidate, Not The Fastest Calendar Slot

Concept: Shift from efficiency to quality. AI interviews create comparable evidence so agencies can defend recommendations to clients.

Mood direction: Case file, evaluation room, polished client report.

Depth layers: BG ghost `EVIDENCE`, MG scorecard stack and candidate ranking, FG checkmarks, transcript excerpt, decision rule.

Animation choreography: Scorecard stack fans in, ranking slots settle, evidence lines type on, best-match marker stamps.

Transition out: Soft focus pull, 0.55s, `sine.inOut`.

### 5. Client-Ready Shortlists In Hours

Concept: Close with the product promise for recruitment agencies: run more searches, protect margins, and send stronger client recommendations.

Mood direction: Premium closing title, restrained and confident.

Depth layers: BG parchment to onyx contrast field, MG Zobo Jobs wordmark and promise, FG pill CTA, URL, fine rule and agency label.

Animation choreography: Wordmark resolves, promise rises, CTA expands, URL types on, final frame breathes.

Transition out: Final fade only.

## Recurring Motifs

Use ledger rules, candidate dossiers, proof ticks, and oversized ghost type across scenes. Keep Deep Onyx as the decisive visual weight and use Slate Echo / Shadowed Steel for secondary operational detail.

## Negative Prompt

Avoid vibrant SaaS gradients, cyan/purple tech colors, cartoon AI imagery, generic dashboard clutter, heavy shadows, over-rounded UI, jump cuts, tiny body text, and vague claims without agency-specific value.
