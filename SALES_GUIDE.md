## The Problem We Solve

First-round screening interviews are the most time-consuming and least valuable part of hiring. Recruiters and hiring managers typically spend **3–5 hours per role** just to identify the 2–3 candidates worth progressing. This creates three compounding problems:

**1. Time cost is enormous**
A recruiter screening 50 applicants for 20 minutes each spends over 16 hours on calls — per role. For a company hiring across 10 roles simultaneously, that's 160+ hours of human time on repetitive first-round conversations.

**2. Inconsistency creates bias**
When different team members conduct different screening calls using different questions, shortlisting decisions are inconsistent. Two equally qualified candidates get evaluated on different criteria, making fair comparison nearly impossible.

**3. Candidate experience suffers**
Most candidates hear nothing for weeks. Companies lose top talent to competitors who move faster. A slow screening process signals a slow company.

**Zobo Jobs eliminates all three problems.**

---

## How It Works — The Simple Version

Zobo Jobs replaces the first-round screening phone call with an AI-powered video interview that runs automatically, any time of day.

```
Step 1: Recruiter creates a job (2 minutes)
        ↓ AI generates a tailored interview script automatically
Step 2: Recruiter shares a unique interview link with candidates
        ↓ Candidates complete the interview on their own time
Step 3: Recruiter reviews a ranked shortlist with scores, video + AI summary
```

That's it. No scheduling. No phone calls. No bias. Just the best candidates, surfaced automatically.

## Feature Guide — What It Does & Why It Matters

### 1. AI-Generated Interview Scripts
**What it does:** The moment a recruiter creates a job (title, description, required skills), the AI automatically generates a complete interview script with 6–8 tailored questions, follow-up prompts, and a scoring rubric — in about 10–15 seconds.

**Why it matters:**
- Recruiters don't need to think about what questions to ask — it's done for them
- Every candidate for the same role is asked the same questions, eliminating inconsistency
- Recruiters can optionally add their own custom questions which the AI weaves in naturally
- The script is calibrated to the experience level required (e.g., a junior vs. senior role gets appropriately levelled questions)

**Value to buyer:** *"You never have to write a screening question again. The AI reads your job description and builds the interview for you."*

---

### 2. The AI Video Interview Experience
**What it does:** Candidates receive a unique link, open it in any browser, grant camera and microphone access, and complete a video interview with Zobo — the AI interviewer. Zobo speaks using a natural, human-sounding voice, listens to the candidate's responses, asks a relevant follow-up question for each topic, then moves to the next question.

**Key experience details:**
- A 3-second countdown countdown prepares the candidate before the interview starts
- The AI acknowledges each answer naturally before responding (sounds like a real phone call)
- After each main question, the AI asks one follow-up to probe deeper
- The AI knows when it's on the last question and delivers a warm, personalised closing
- If the candidate turns off their camera, the interview pauses with a clear message — recording integrity is maintained
- The entire session (camera + audio) is recorded as a single video file

**Why it matters:**
- Candidates can interview at 11pm or 6am — no scheduling friction
- The experience feels human and professional, not robotic — protecting the company's employer brand
- Every candidate goes through the exact same structured process

**Value to buyer:** *"Your candidates get a consistent, professional interview experience available 24/7. You never miss a good candidate because of a scheduling conflict."*

---

### 3. Automated Candidate Scoring
**What it does:** The moment an interview is completed, the AI evaluates the full conversation against the job requirements and produces a score across five dimensions:

| Dimension | What it measures |
|---|---|
| **Technical Knowledge** | Does the candidate know the required skills? |
| **Communication** | Are they clear, articulate, and easy to understand? |
| **Problem Solving** | Can they think through challenges logically? |
| **Experience Fit** | Does their background match what the role needs? |
| **Confidence** | Do they present themselves with appropriate conviction? |

An overall score (0–100) is calculated as a weighted average, and the AI produces a `Recommended / Not Recommended` verdict.

**Why it matters:**
- Removes subjectivity from shortlisting decisions
- Makes it defensible — scores are based on what was actually said, not how someone felt about a voice
- Every candidate is assessed on identical criteria, every time

**Value to buyer:** *"Instead of gut feel, your shortlist is backed by a consistent, objective scoring model tied directly to your job requirements."*

---

### 4. Video Recording & Playback
**What it does:** The entire interview session — face and voice — is recorded and stored securely. Hiring managers can watch the full interview on the candidate review page at any time.

**Why it matters:**
- Hiring managers who weren't on the "call" can still evaluate the candidate
- Useful for panel decisions — share the video link with team members
- Eliminates the game of telephone where a recruiter summarises a call and loses nuance
- Creates an audit trail if a hiring decision is ever questioned

**Value to buyer:** *"Your hiring manager can watch the actual interview in 10 minutes instead of sitting on a 30-minute screening call."*

---

### 5. AI Summary, Strengths & Weaknesses
**What it does:** Alongside the scores, the AI produces:
- A 2–3 sentence plain-language summary of the candidate
- A list of key strengths (specific to what was said in the interview)
- A list of areas to improve or watch out for

**Why it matters:**
- A hiring manager can read the summary in 30 seconds and decide whether to watch the video
- Strengths and weaknesses are specific and evidence-based — not generic
- Makes it easy to compare candidates side-by-side without watching multiple videos

**Value to buyer:** *"In the time it takes to read three paragraphs, you know whether a candidate is worth your time."*

---

### 6. Full Interview Transcript
**What it does:** Every word spoken in the interview — by the AI and the candidate — is transcribed and displayed in a clean chat-style view on the candidate review page.

**Why it matters:**
- Searchable — hiring managers can scan for specific keywords or topics
- Useful for candidates who may have had audio quality issues
- Supports accessibility and compliance requirements

---

### 7. Shareable Interview Link + Email Invites
**What it does:** Each job has a unique interview link that can be:
- Shared directly (copy and paste into any message)
- Emailed to individual candidates from within the platform
- Bulk-sent by uploading a CSV of candidate email addresses

The platform also sends automated reminder emails at 24 hours and 48 hours to candidates who haven't completed their interview yet.

**Why it matters:**
- Zero friction to get candidates into the process — no app to download, no account to create
- Works with any existing workflow (LinkedIn DM, job board, email — just share the link)
- Automated reminders recover candidates who started but didn't finish

**Value to buyer:** *"Share a link anywhere. Candidates click it and interview. No back-and-forth emails, no Calendly, no phone tags."*

---

### 8. Recruiter Dashboard
**What it does:** A clean management dashboard showing:
- All active jobs and their candidate counts
- Completion rates across all roles
- Candidate rankings within each job (sorted by score)
- Status of every candidate (pending, completed, interrupted)
- Top 3 candidates surfaced automatically per role

**Why it matters:**
- A hiring manager can see the state of every role at a glance
- Top candidates are surfaced immediately — no need to manually sort through results
- Shows who hasn't completed their interview yet (candidates can be followed up with)

---

### 9. Interview Interruption Handling
**What it does:** If a candidate loses internet connection or closes the session unexpectedly:
- The session is automatically marked as "Interrupted"
- Whatever transcript was collected is saved
- If the candidate answered at least 2 questions, a partial AI evaluation is generated
- The recruiter sees an "Interrupted" badge on the candidate page with all available data and a clear note explaining what happened

**Why it matters:**
- Recruiters are never left with a blank profile — even incomplete sessions yield data
- Protects against false negatives — a candidate with a connection issue isn't silently discarded

## Common Objections & How to Handle Them

### "Candidates won't like being interviewed by an AI."
> *"That's a fair instinct, but the experience is designed to feel like a real phone call — the AI speaks naturally, acknowledges answers, asks follow-ups, and closes warmly. Most candidates find it less stressful than a live screening call because there's no scheduling pressure and they can do it at a time that suits them. We're not replacing the human relationship — we're replacing the administrative first filter so the human relationship starts at the right stage."*

---

### "We worry about missing good candidates who perform badly in AI interviews."
> *"Two things here. First, if a candidate struggles to communicate clearly in a structured AI conversation, that's usually signal, not noise — communication is a real job requirement. Second, you're not making a hire based on this — you're just deciding who gets to the second round. The AI surfaces the top performers; you still meet every candidate you want to progress."*

---

### "Our roles are too specialised for a generic AI."
> *"The AI isn't generic — it reads your specific job description, required skills, and experience level to generate the questions. If you hire for niche roles, you can also add your own custom questions which the AI integrates naturally. Every interview is built for that specific role."*

---

### "What about data privacy and GDPR?"
> *"Candidate video recordings and transcripts are stored securely. Access is restricted to the company that ran the interview — candidates' data is not shared with or sold to third parties. For enterprise clients, we can discuss specific data residency and deletion requirements."*

---

### "We already use an ATS / recruitment platform."
> *"Zobo Jobs works alongside any ATS — you can paste the interview link into any job posting, email, or LinkedIn message. Candidates don't need a Zobo account. The output (scores, transcript, video) lives in Zobo and you use it to decide who to advance in your existing workflow. We're not replacing your ATS — we're plugging into the screening stage."*

---

### "What if the candidate's internet drops during the interview?"
> *"We handle that. If a candidate disconnects, the session is automatically marked as interrupted, whatever transcript was collected is saved, and if they answered at least two questions, the AI runs a partial evaluation. The recruiter can see exactly what happened and what data was collected — they're never left with a blank profile."*

## Glossary — Terms to Know

| Term | Plain-language meaning |
|---|---|
| **Interview Script** | The set of questions the AI will ask for a specific job. Generated automatically. |
| **Interview Link** | The unique URL shared with candidates to access their interview |
| **TTS (Text-to-Speech)** | The technology that converts the AI's words into natural spoken audio (ElevenLabs) |
| **STT (Speech-to-Text)** | The technology that transcribes what the candidate says into text (OpenAI Whisper) |
| **Overall Score** | A 0–100 weighted score across all five evaluation dimensions |
| **Recommendation** | The AI's verdict: Recommended or Not Recommended for the next stage |
| **Interrupted Interview** | A session that ended before completion due to disconnection or tab close |
| **Custom Questions** | Questions a recruiter adds when creating a job that the AI incorporates |
